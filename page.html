Accessories Review:

Knog 4v

by Dennis Bean-Larson 1/15/2013

This isn't the first Knog light that we're ridden with. I fact it's the 4rd generation that we've charged and ridden with. Every one has been a winner but the Blinder Series win from every angle. Rechargeable .... Gorgeous to look at .... Bright Bright Bright .... and the newest 4V is no exception.

The 4V has the familiar stretchy silicone that Knog uses for the easiest attachment method couple with the same clever buckle system of the other's in the Blinder Series. Clips on easily, clips off when you park your bike.

The Blinder 4V has five different flash modes and I was surprised to read that the Eco-Mode could last up to fifty hours! Wow, as soon as I read that I had to go get the 4V and see which flash mode that wasâ€¦ I mean 50 hours is a long time.

There's a delay built into the on-off button so you can't accidentially turn it on and the same button toggles you thru the diffent flash modes.

No more batteries with the current Blinder Series, simply flip up the rear panel and up pops the USB connection.

Complete with Knog logo.........

The 4V USB plug made me nervous at first, it's a little awkward to plug into your computer or other device. Knog doesn't provide any connector cord. I'd been confused even with the first set, and it wasn't until I was writing this review and looked at the photos and saw that little USB "pitchfork" that I realized which way to plug it in. I've had to try is both ways....I mean it's not like I've got to plug the light in every night.

Plug it in, keep it in your pocket. It's easy-on easy-off. If you're a real tech person, Knog provides plenty to keep you reading for awhile. It's just one of the reasons that Knog is the real innovator in the bike light world.

Knog Blinder 4V $44.95 Retail
